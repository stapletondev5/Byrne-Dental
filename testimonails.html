Bleaching

Bleaching is a process that allows the teeth to become whiter and which is controlled by the patient at home. This is a very simple technique and solution to a need for many patients which is to have whiter looking teeth without undergoing extensive treatment.

On the first visit, an assessment is needed, the teeth are cleaned if necessary and impressions are taken . A few days later, the bleaching trays which have been custom made in the laboratory are supplied to the patient with the bleaching gel . It is required to wear the trays overnight for approximately 2 weeks until the desired colour change is achieved. The trays are light and thin and easy to wear. Overnight wearing means the minimumsocial disruption.

Bridge

A Bridge is a technique used to restore a space where a tooth is missing or has been extracted. There are many ways to restore a missing tooth space, a denture, an implant andwhere appropriate, a bridge . A bridge is an excellent choice, fixed in place and very cosmetic and functional. There are 2 types, a “Maryland” adhesive bridge and a conventional bridge. The first one is used in cases where one front tooth is missing and involves minimal or no alteration to the adjacent teeth. 2 visits are required, an impression is made on the first visit , sent to the laboratory and fitted 2 weeks later. This is very suitable for patients that are wearing a partial denture to replace a missing tooth. This allows these patients to have an affordable fixed bridge with minimal visits and invasion. A conventional bridge is used where a “Maryland“ bridge is not suitable, where there is more than one tooth missing ,to replace back teeth and where implants are not appropriate . The procedure is similar but a small amount of tooth reduction has to be carried out at the first visit. Temporary crowns are placed in between visits.
